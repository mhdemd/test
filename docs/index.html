    Allameh Mohaddes Nouri University

    In this research project, an in-depth investigation was conducted on the behavior of nickel-titanium shape memory alloy, focusing on its unique properties such as shape memory, superelasticity, and energy absorption capabilities during cyclic loading. A neuro-fuzzy model was developed using MATLAB R2013b to accurately capture the material’s response under various loading conditions across a frequency range of 0.05 to 2 Hz and within five different strain intervals. The behavioral model was then implemented as a mass damper spring in a dynamic system subjected to seismic accelerations simulating earthquake conditions, providing valuable insights into the effectiveness of the shape memory alloy in mitigating seismic effects.
